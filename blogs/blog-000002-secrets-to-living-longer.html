  1. Eat healthier
    • There are many common misconceptions about eating healthy. One, it costs too much. Two, it's too much work and so on. Stop making excuses and start taking care of your body for a change. Don’t we all want to live longer?
    • This one’s pretty obvious but how to actually eat healthy is so foreign to many. Diets rich in fruits and vegetables, low-fat dairy and low-wholegrains(link to gluten free blog) lower the risk of many heart related and obesity related diseases by maintaining your body's levels of blood pressure and cholesterol-caused by high levels of salt and saturated fats. Eating a healthy portion of fish like salmon or trout at least once a week also reduces the risks for heart-diseases, since the omega fatty acids help to keep your heart in tip-top shape! Calcium also ensures that your bones stay healthy and strong, even preventing bone loss!
  2. Stay Active
    • It's no secret that exercising boosts life expectancy. Staying physically active adds years to life span and keeps you looking and feeling healthy! Even 15 minutes of exercise a day can provide many benefits and add 2-3 years onto your life. How to exercise:
      1. First off start small and be realistic. Don't just start working out for hours a day. This way you wont get discouraged about your progress.
      2. Try working out for just 150 minutes a week. After you gain more confidence and stamina, turn the 150 minutes to 250, 300 and so on.
      3. Plan ahead to ensure that you have enough time to get in those essential hours of exercise every week.
  3. Avoid smoking and alcohol
    • It's a no-brainer that smoking and excessive alcohol damages your health.
    • Alcohol
      Try avoiding the bottled killer to reduce chances of alcohol poisoning, cancer, heart damage, liver failure which eventually leads to death. Heavy alcohol consumption is directly linked to cancer, heart and liver damage. Alcohol scars and damages the liver which interferes with the liver’s ability to metabolize the toxic chemical. This in turn results in fat accumulation on the organ- ultimately leading to hepatitis or cirrhosis as the liver starts to have organ failure when the filtration of blood stops which creates organ failure throughout the body. Alcohol also damages the heart-leading to heart failure due to high blood pressure.
    • Smoking
      Smoking is known to have many health effects such as increased chance of stroke, coronary heart disease and other cardiovascular issues making it the leading killer in many countries worldwide. Smoking damages blood vessels, making them grow thinner and thicker- increasing the chance of blood clots and high blood pressure. I know what you're thinking, a few cigarettes won’t hurt- well yes they will. Even people who smoke less than 5 cigarettes a day already have a higher chance of developing cardiovascular diseases. Don’t gamble with your life. Make the change and take a step in the right direction.
    • Try the following:
      1. Give yourself a pep talk.
      2. Motivate yourself.
      3. Hang around with non-smokers.
      4. Stop after the first drink.
      5. It may be difficult at first but nothing is easy.
  4. Create a sleeping schedule that works for YOU!
    • Sleeping is essential for your body to function and cells to reproduce. Believe it or not but lack of sleep can reduce your lifespan. Not enough sleep causes inflammation and increases your chance of developing heart diseases, obesity and diabetes. Ensure that you get between 6-8 hours of sleep, no little than 5 hours and not more than 10 hours. Excessive sleep can also cause many problems for yourself-shortening your lifespan along the way. To ensure that your REM cycle is in check, go to bed and wake up at the same time everyday.
    • How to sleep better:
      1. Go to bed at the same time and wake up at the same time.
      2. Control your exposure to light by reducing the amount of time spent on your phone or electronics before bedtime. Read a book instead!
      3. Exercise daily to improve symptoms of insomnia and sleep apnea. The more tired your body is, the better quality of sleep you get.
      4. Avoid caffeine and nicotine
      5. Avoid large meals and alcohol close to bedtime
      6. Cut back on carbs and sugar as they tend to result in more wakefulness during the night.
  5. Block out the unnecessary stress from daily life
    • Many things result in stress and anxiety, whether it's work or just being in public. Chronic anxiety and stress significantly decreases your lifespan. Studies show that women are more likely to develop stress than men. 28% of women and 20% of men have reported having high levels of stress.Stress is inevitable but there are many ways to lessen the weight on your shoulders.
      1. Spend more time with family and friends:being with friends and family gives you a sense of belonging and uplifts your mood.
      2. Laugh: watch funny tv shows or hang out with friends. Laughing is known to relieve stress and tension instantly.
      3. Try deep breathing:Your nervous system activates when you go into stress causing stress hormones to release, increase heart rate breathing and constrict blood vessels. Breathing exercises.
        1. Close your eyes.
        2. Take a deep breath.
        3. Breathe in an breathe out. Imagine the tension and stress leave your body.
        4. Continue for 10 to 20 minutes.
      4. Listen to calming music: nature sounds and certain types of music having a calming effect on the mind and body.
